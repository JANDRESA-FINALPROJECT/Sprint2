About the book

The Recipe Book!



Back in the teenage years of my mother, as a bread winner, she worked in Manila for almost two years. In the span of 2 years, she experienced being enrolled in one of the baking schools in Manila for six months through her employer. And through those months, she managed to keep the list of recipes she learned. Make use of it, as well as, treasure it, which makes it last after almost 29 years. Thus, sharing you some bit of it.

Ingredients

Banana Cupcake

2 cups all purpose flour
1 tsp baking soda
1 tsp salt
1/3 cup oil
2 maya farm eggs
2/3 cup milk with 1 tsp vinegar
1 cup banana





Buko Sherbet

2 1/4 cups water
1 1/4 cups sugar
1 85-grams pack unflavored gelatin
1/2 cup buko juice
1 1/2 cups milk
1.2 cup buko meat (shredded)
2 maya farm eggs (egg white)
1/4 cup sugar




Bibingkang Malagkit

2 cups malagkit
1 1/2 cups brown sugar
3/4 cup coconut milk
3 1/2 cup diluted coconut milk
1 tsp salt
1.4 tsp powdered anise






Sinigang na Hipon

1/4 cup onion (sliced)
1/4 cup tomatoes (chopped)
10 pcs camias
6 cups rice wine water
1/2 kg fresh shrimp
1 tsp salt
1 tsp MSG
2 cups kangkong leaves and tender stalks



Chicken Adobo

1 lb chicken
3/4 cup vinegar
2Tbsp cooking oil
1 Tbsp black pepper
salt to taste
1 head garlic (pounded)
2 Tbsp soy sauce
2 Tbsp water




Lechon Kawali

34 ounces water
1 kg liempo (pork belly)
3 clove garlic or 1 small head
2 Tbsp salt
cooking oil







Kare-kare

1 pork pata ( 1 1/2 - 2 kgs)
3 Tbs oil
2 Tbs atsuete
8 cloves garlic (minced)
1 medium size onion
1/4 cup bagoong alamang
1/3 cup ground peanuts
1/3 cup ground toasted rice 2 pcs eggplants
1 bundle string beans (cut into 3 each)
1 small puso ng saging (sliced)

Kilawing Puso ng Saging

2-3 cups banana blossoms (chopped)
1 Tbs garlic (minced)
1 medium onion (sliced)
6 Tbs vinegar
3 pcs green chili pepper (sliced)







Cabbage Roll

1 head cabbage (about 1/2 kg)
1/4 kg ground beef
1/4 kg ground pork
1 onion (minced)
1 1/2 tsp salt
1/4 tsp pepper
1/4 tsp oregano
1 cup tomato sauce / fresh tomato (chopped)



Procedure


Banana Cupcake



1. Preheat oven to 350 fahrenheit
2. Grease the muffin pan or the muffin cups
3. set aside
4. Sift and mix first the four ingredients, all purpose flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt, in the bowl
5. Blend oil and sugar
6. Add eggs one at a time and beat until smooth
7. Add flour mixture alternately with milk
8. Stir in bananas
9. Bake until golden brown

Buko Sherbet



1. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar and gelatin
2. Heat to boiling while stirring occasionally
3. Lower heat and simmer for 5 minutes
4. Remove from the fire and stir buko juice, strain and cool
5. Stir in milk and buko meat
6. Pour into loaf pan
7. Freeze for about 4 hours, stirring once in a while
8. Beat egg whites until frothy
9. Gradually add sugar and continue beating until stiff
10. Beat gelatin mixture until soft
11. Fold in beaten egg white
12. Return to loaf pan and freeze until firm

Bibingkang Malagkit



1. Boil the diluted coconut milk in carajay.
2. Add malagkit and salt
3. Boil until quite dry
4. Stirring constantly to keep from burning
5. Lower the heat and add 2/3 cup sugar
6. Ready the clay oven or bibingkahan with wilted banana leaf
7. Pour the mixture into it
8. Pour the rich cocconut milk and the rest of the sugar on top of malagkit
9. Cook it until brown

Sinigang na Hipon



1. Saute onions and tomatoes
2. Add the camias and cook until tender and mushy
3. Add rice water and let it boil
4. When boiling, put the shrimp and season with salt and MSG
5. Add kangkong and cook for 2 minutes.

Chicken Adobo



1. Combine chicken, garlic and soy sauce in the bowl and mix well
2. Prepare the cooking pan, heat it and add cooking oil
3. Pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes < br> 4. Pour in the remaining marinade and water
5. Bring it to boil
6. Simmer for until the chicken gets tender (mostly 30 minutes)
6. Add vinegar and cook again for another 10 minutes
7. Put in the sugar and salt and stir it.
8. Wait until cook

Lechon Kawali



1. Pour water on pot
2. Add the liempo, garlic, and 1 Tbsp of salt
3. Remove the meat and let it cool for few minutes
4. Spread the remaining salt on the liempo
5. Heat oil on the pot
6. Deep fry the meat and fry until crispy
7. Remove the meat in the pot
8. Place in the rack and let it cool

Kare-kare



1. Cut pata into smaller pieces
2. Boil in water until tender
3. drain pata and set aside the broth
4. Heat oil and add atsuete
5. Stir until the colors come out
6. Discard the seeds then saute the garlic and onion
7. add bagoong, grpund peanuts and rice
8. Stir in the broth and bring to a boil
9. Add pata and cook for few minutes
10. Add vegetables and cook until tender

Kilawing Puso ng Saging



1. Prepare bowl with water and salt
2. Slice the puso and drop into bowl
3. Saute garlic, onion
4. Add puso, simmer until boil and tender
5. Add vinegar and bring it to boil
6. Add water and chili pepper and wait until boil
7. Season with salt and pepper

Cabbage Roll



1. Boil cabbage for 5 minutes
2. Remove from water and separate the leaves
3. Mix remaining ingredients except the tomato
4. Fill leaves with the mixture and roll
5. Fasten with toothpick
6. Arrange in saucepan and pour the tomato
7. Cover and simmer for about 1 1/2 hours.
